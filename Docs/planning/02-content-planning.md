## Writing Guidelines

### Tone & Voice

**General Principles:**
- **Professional yet approachable**: Confident without being arrogant
- **Clear and concise**: Respect the reader's time
- **Action-oriented**: Use active voice
- **Honest**: Don't oversell or undersell yourself
- **Specific**: Use concrete examples and metrics

**Section-Specific Tone:**

**Landing Page:** Warm, welcoming, authentic
- "Hi, I'm Joshuaâ€”I bridge the gap between business strategy and technical execution."

**Business Section:** Professional, results-driven, strategic
- "Led cross-functional team to deliver 23% efficiency improvement in Q3 2024."

**Developer Section:** Technical, creative, problem-solving focused
- "Built a full-stack therapy application using Next.js, integrating OpenAI API for intelligent conversations."

**Projects Section:** Balanced, storytelling, reflective
- "This project taught me the importance of user research before diving into development."

### Content Structure Templates

#### About Me (Landing Page)

**Paragraph 1:** Who you are professionally
```
I'm Joshua Nizamudin, a business analyst and web developer who bridges the gap 
between strategic thinking and technical execution. Currently pursuing my degree 
in [Major] at NJIT, I've discovered that the best solutions come from understanding 
both the business problem and the technical possibilities.
```

**Paragraph 2:** Your journey/background
```
My journey into technology began with [experience/moment], which sparked my interest 
in [area]. Since then, I've combined my business education with hands-on web 
development, working on projects ranging from [type] to [type].
```

**Paragraph 3:** What you do/specialize in
```
I specialize in creating web applications that solve real business problems. Whether 
it's building a therapy app with AI integration or optimizing business processes 
through automation, I focus on delivering measurable results that matter to users 
and stakeholders alike.
```

**Paragraph 4:** What drives you/future goals
```
What excites me most is the intersection of business strategy and technology. I'm 
constantly exploring how emerging technologies can create business value, and I'm 
looking for opportunities to contribute to teams that value both strategic thinking 
and technical excellence.
```

#### Project Description Template

**Structure:**
1. **Hook/Overview** (1-2 sentences): What is it and why it matters
2. **The Challenge** (2-3 sentences): What problem did this solve?
3. **My Role** (1-2 sentences): What did you specifically do?
4. **The Approach** (2-3 sentences): How did you solve it?
5. **Key Features** (bullet points): 3-5 main features
6. **Results/Impact** (1-2 sentences): What was the outcome?
7. **Technologies Used** (list): Tech stack
8. **Links**: Live demo, GitHub repo

**Example:**

```markdown
## IS218 AI Therapy Application

A comprehensive mental health support application featuring 8 specialized AI therapist 
personas, each representing a different evidence-based therapeutic approach. This 
project demonstrates professional software development practices including TDD, 
clean architecture, and enterprise-grade logging.

**The Challenge:** Mental health support is often inaccessible or stigmatized. I 
wanted to create a safe, judgment-free space where users could explore different 
therapeutic approaches and receive support powered by AI, while demonstrating 
production-quality development practices.

**My Role:** Solo developer responsible for full-stack development, from system 
architecture to UI/UX design. Implemented authentication, intake assessment, 
session management, and AI integration with OpenAI's GPT-4.

**Key Features:**
- 8 specialized therapist personas with unique therapeutic approaches
- Intelligent intake assessment with weighted scoring algorithm
- User authentication and personalized session history
- Beautiful toast-themed UI with responsive design
- 100% test coverage using TDD methodology

**Results:** Successfully deployed application demonstrating both technical expertise 
and understanding of user-centered design. Project showcased in course as example 
of professional development practices.

**Technologies:** Next.js, React, Python, Streamlit, OpenAI API, pytest, JSON storage

[Live Demo](#) | [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/NizaJ27/IS218-AI-Demo)
```

#### Case Study Deep-Dive Template

For detailed case studies (800-1200 words), use this structure:

**1. Executive Summary** (100 words)
- Project overview
- Your role
- Key achievement

**2. Background & Context** (150-200 words)
- Business/user problem
- Why this mattered
- Constraints and requirements

**3. Research & Discovery** (150-200 words)
- How you understood the problem
- Research methods used
- Key insights gained

**4. Ideation & Planning** (150-200 words)
- Potential solutions considered
- Why you chose your approach
- Technical and business considerations

**5. Implementation** (200-300 words)
- How you built it
- Technical architecture
- Key challenges and how you overcame them
- Tools and technologies used

**6. Results & Impact** (100-150 words)
- Quantitative metrics (if available)
- Qualitative feedback
- Business/user value delivered

**7. Reflections** (100-150 words)
- What you learned
- What you'd do differently
- Skills gained

## Content Gathering Workflow

### Phase 1: Inventory (Week 1)

**Day 1-2: Collect Basic Information**
1. List all projects you've worked on (don't filter yet)
2. Gather project files, screenshots, repositories
3. Find work experience documentation
4. Locate academic records and transcripts
5. Collect any existing bios or summaries

**Day 3-4: Organize by Category**
1. Sort projects into categories:
   - Business/Strategy projects
   - Web development projects
   - Academic projects
   - Personal projects
2. Identify which projects showcase both business and technical skills
3. Select top 10-15 projects for portfolio

**Day 5: Draft Outline**
1. Write bullet points for each project (don't write full descriptions yet)
2. Note key technologies used
3. Identify measurable results or metrics
4. Determine which projects need detailed case studies

### Phase 2: Writing (Week 2-3)

**Landing Page (2-3 hours)**
1. Write about me paragraphs
2. Craft hero headline and tagline
3. Write navigation card descriptions
4. Define quick stats

**Business Section (4-5 hours)**
1. Write professional summary
2. Document all work experience
3. List and categorize business skills
4. Write 3-5 business project descriptions
5. Document academic achievements

**Developer Section (5-6 hours)**
1. Write developer introduction
2. Inventory tech stack
3. Write descriptions for 10-15 projects
4. Draft "Why I Built It" for each project
5. Write 3 technical case studies

**Integrated Portfolio (3-4 hours)**
1. Compile all projects
2. Write hybrid case studies (business + technical perspective)
3. Create project timeline

**Contact Page (1 hour)**
1. Write "let's work together" message
2. Define availability and status
3. List contact preferences

### Phase 3: Review & Polish (Week 4)

**Self-Review Checklist:**
- [ ] All content is grammatically correct
- [ ] Tone is consistent within each section
- [ ] No typos or spelling errors
- [ ] All claims are accurate and honest
- [ ] Metrics and dates are correct
- [ ] Links work and go to correct destinations
- [ ] Content is scannable (good headings, bullet points)

**Peer Review:**
- Ask 2-3 friends or colleagues to review
- Get feedback on clarity and authenticity
- Verify technical accuracy
- Test if content resonates with different audiences

## Image & Media Guidelines

### Required Images

**Profile Photo:**
- Professional headshot or casual professional photo
- High resolution (at least 1000x1000px)
- Good lighting, clean background
- Friendly, approachable expression
- Format: JPG or PNG

**Project Screenshots:**
- Desktop: 1920x1080px or 1440x900px
- Mobile: 375x667px or 390x844px
- Show key features and UI
- Clean, no Lorem Ipsum if possible
- Include both light and dark mode if applicable

**Project Thumbnails:**
- Consistent size: 800x600px or 16:9 ratio
- Eye-catching, representative of project
- High quality, compressed for web

### Image Optimization

1. **Compress all images** using TinyPNG or Squoosh
2. **Target file size**: < 200KB for full screenshots, < 50KB for thumbnails
3. **Use WebP format** with JPG fallback
4. **Always include alt text** describing the image
5. **Organize in folders** by project or section

## Resume Best Practices

### Resume Structure

**Header:**
- Name (large, prominent)
- Professional title/headline
- Contact information (email, phone, location, LinkedIn, GitHub)

**Professional Summary:**
- 3-4 sentences capturing who you are
- Highlight key skills and value proposition
- Mention both business and technical expertise

**Experience:**
- Reverse chronological order (most recent first)
- Company, Position, Location, Dates
- 3-5 bullet points per role
- Start with action verbs (Led, Developed, Implemented, Analyzed)
- Include quantifiable achievements

**Education:**
- Degree, Major, Institution, Graduation Date
- GPA (if 3.5+)
- Relevant coursework
- Academic honors

**Skills:**
- Categorize: Business Skills, Technical Skills, Tools
- List specific technologies and frameworks
- Be honest about proficiency levels

**Certifications/Awards** (if applicable)

### Resume Writing Tips

**Action Verbs to Use:**
- Business: Led, Analyzed, Optimized, Streamlined, Coordinated
- Technical: Developed, Built, Implemented, Designed, Architected
- Results: Increased, Decreased, Improved, Achieved, Delivered
